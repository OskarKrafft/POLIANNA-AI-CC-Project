Article 99
Identification of sharks
1.   Without prejudice to Article 98, beheading and skinning of sharks on board and before landing shall be prohibited. Beheaded and skinned sharks shall not be marketed at the first sale markets after landing.
2.   Without prejudice to Article 98, Member States shall ensure that sharks are kept on board, transhipped, landed and marketed at first sale in such a way that species are recognisable and identifiable, and that the catch, incidental catch and, whenever appropriate, release of those species can be monitored and recorded.
